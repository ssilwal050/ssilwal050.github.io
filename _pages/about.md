## About Me

I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Mathematical Sciences at **Michigan Technological University**, Houghton, MI, USA. I am working under the supervision of [Prof. Xiao Zhang](https://www.mtu.edu/math/department/faculty-staff/faculty/x-zhang/). My research focuses on **Bayesian modelling for discrete data** — specifically developing MCMC methods for binary, ordinal, and nominal measures with missing values using the **Multinomial Probit Model (MNP)**. Prior to my doctoral studies, I worked as a data analyst and statistician on large-scale survey projects funded by international organizations including the World Bank and NORC. I am a member of the **American Statistical Association** and have served as President of the Nepali Student Organization at Michigan Tech.

---

## Research Interests

- **Bayesian Computation for Latent Variable Models**: Developing efficient MCMC samplers for discrete outcome models, with a focus on parameter-expanded data augmentation strategies.
- **Missing Data Imputation**: Bayesian methods to handle missing values in multivariate discrete settings using the MNP framework.
- **Multivariate Data Analysis**: Multivariate regression and latent variable models with real-world data applications, including longitudinal/panel extensions.
- **Discrete & Categorical Models**: Binary, ordinal, and nominal outcome models with applications in survey data and biostatistics.

---

## Current Work

I am currently developing **MCMC methods for binary, ordinal, and nominal measures with missing values** using the Multinomial Probit Model (MNP). Key contributions include:

- Developed a **parameter-expanded (PX) data-augmentation Gibbs sampler** that improves posterior mixing, convergence, and estimation accuracy for nominal outcomes.
- Extended the MNP framework to **multivariate and longitudinal (panel) settings** with integrated missing data handling.
- Conducting simulation studies and real data applications to validate the proposed methods.
